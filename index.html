My Top 10 Video Games From Childhood

10. Sonic Adventure

This is the first open-world sonic game.This game actually came out before GTA III, so this could be the first open-world game. This particular open-world game was very new and a head of the time and sonic was actually very popular. To be able to travel across the sonic world and do missions was a dream for a young me.

9. Shenmue

This was another open world game ahead of its time. I just remember walking the streets of Yokosuka, walking door to door knocking in hopes to find any clues to find my father's murderer. Although I didn't enjoy some of the quick-time events, the adventure was unforgettable. This was the first time I completely felt immersed in a video game.

8. The Matrix: Path of Neo

Oooh, the endless combos. This game I only rented but I had it for sooo long we had a huge fee to pay to blockbuster. I played this game over and over and over and over. It felt like each time I played it I saw something new. I always wanted to know what it would be like to be Neo in a video game and this game did a great job depicting it.

7. Powerstone 2

Ok, Powerstone was Super Smash Brothers before Super Smash Brothers. It is possible that this series inspired Super Smash or that they inspired each other. This was plenty of fun alone and with friends. I could never forget re-playing the demo countless times.

6. Fable

Fable was like no other game I played. It was short but the replay value was high. At that time, I always thought the longer the game is, the more impressive. This game taught me that there is beauty is a short story.

The level of detail & possibilities in this game was incredible. You could get married, grow old, be evil, and get scarrs in battles. I found a new appreciation for detail in video games thanks to this game.

5. Prince of Persia The Sands Of Time

I just remember waking up one Christmas and unwraping this game. The rest is pretty much history. I loved games that manipulated time but this game somehow appeared to do it differently. Rewind your death and try to get it right the second time or use slow motion to land a far jump. I appreciated the cerebral aspect of the game.

4. Starfox Adventures

I have fond memories of playing this game. I just remember playing for hours. Exploring the vast worlds and fighting armor clad reptiles. I think this might be one of the games fans couldn't stand because it wasn't normal Starfox but honestly, I wouldn't play a normal Starfox game. I did appreciate this new spin on the series.

3. Super Mario Sunshine

This game still holds up today. There are many awesome Mario adventures but for some reason this is the only mario game I was able to complete. I enjoyed using all of the different water powered gadgets given to me. Somehow this game managed to make cleaning fun. If that isn't amazing I don't know what is.

2. GTA San Adreas

This is the only game I've played at the time that truly simulated real life. You could make the character eat, drink, workout, and all of these had an effect on your character. If you ate too much you could look just like the third photo. I couldn't believe the features in the game.

1. Knights Of The Old Republic (KOTOR)

The game of complete FREEDOM! I love a game when everytime you play it something completely different can happen. You could even betray your own team if you wanted. I put this game at number one because it still holds up after all these years. The story was great, the combat was fulfilling, and most of all the freedom to choose every thing in the game. This game had no hand holding. There aren't many games like these anymore and for that reason it will always be in my heart.
